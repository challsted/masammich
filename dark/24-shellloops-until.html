Shell Loops - until

A quick cover on a rarely used but still important loop

Until Loops!

What is a "loop"?

They are called this because they "loop" or iterate though data or instructions until they are no longer true. To be able to understand what I mean by "until they are no longer true", you must understand what is known as "Boolean Logic". Boolean logic (very simplified) is a "true or false" "1 or 0", basically, its always one way or the other.

What is an "until" loop?

A until loop is a lot like a while loop. As in they repeate an action over and over and over. Where they differ is that a while loop does something while the statement is true (could also be said "While the statment is true"). An until loop preform an action over and over until it is true.

Here is an example until loop:

a=1
until [ $a -gt 6 ]
do
  echo "\"A\" is equal to $a"
  a=`exec $a + 1`
done
This will not print anyting until a is greater then 6 (so starting at 7).
If you plan on running this, script, note that there is no end to it (infinite loop) so you'll have to ctrl+c to exit the loop
